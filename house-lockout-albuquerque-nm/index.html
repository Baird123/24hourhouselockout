Frequently Asked Questions

What is the "1/4 rule" for residential lock maintenance?

To prevent sticking, we recommend applying a dry lubricant to your locks every quarter (1/4 of the year). This keeps the internal pins moving freely and prevents the "frozen" cylinders common in the climate.

Is it better to rekey or replace my locks after moving?

If your hardware is in good condition, a rekey is usually better. It’s more cost-effective and ensures that old keys from previous owners no longer work, while keeping your high-quality handlesets in place.

Can you extract a key that broke off inside the lock?

Yes. Using specialized extraction tools, we can usually remove the broken blade without having to replace the entire lock, saving you time and money.

Do you provide master key systems for multi-family properties?

Absolutely. We can design a system where a single master key opens all common areas and individual units, while residents' keys only open their specific doors.

Will my homeowner's insurance cover a lockout?

Many policies include a "roadside assistance" style clause for home lockouts. We provide full digital receipts and manifests that you can submit to your provider for potential reimbursement.

Can you open high-security or 'smart' deadbolts?

Yes. While these are designed to be pick-resistant, our technicians carry specialized tools to bypass or reset most electronic and high-security residential systems without destroying the unit.
